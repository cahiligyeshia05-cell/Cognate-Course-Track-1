This Laboratory Exercise (1.1) introduced the student to a basic circuit layout which utilized Arduino. It applies classroom and textbook discussions on a drafting platform that presents a clear visual demonstration of an electric diagram alongside the project's simulation. In terms of hardware, this exercise employed 12 LED lights, each connected to a respective resistor, and wired from pins 2 to 13. The ground pin and corresponding LED lights were also connected to the negative terminals of the breadboard.

In terms of software, the exercise specifically required for the LED lights to light up from left to right, then right to left, and repeat. The code utilized for loops, looping through the leftmost pin (pin 13) to the rightmost pin (pin 2). Another loop proceeds, this time from the rightmost to the leftmost pin. These processes continue to repeat with a 100-millisecond delay for each "on" and "off" operation to achieve the desired output.
